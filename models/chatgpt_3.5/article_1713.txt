In recent years, there's been growing interest in complex-valued neural networks in computer science. These networks use complex numbers to tackle certain tasks, like classification, which traditional real-valued networks struggle with. The article compares complex and real-valued networks, finding that complex networks perform better in specialized architectures or when dealing with complex input data. They tested five different activation functions to see which works best for complex networks, discovering intriguing results that could shape the future of neural network design. Overall, this research sheds light on the potential of complex-valued networks in solving real-world problems, offering exciting possibilities for the future of computer science.
